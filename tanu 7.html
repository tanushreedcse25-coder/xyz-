About Sam Manekshaw

Sam Manekshaw was born on 3rd of april in 1914.He served as the Chief of Army Staff of the Indian Army and is best known for leading India to victory in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971.

Achievements

  • First Indian Army officer to be promoted to Field Marshal (India’s highest army rank)
  • Chief of Army Staff during the Indo‑Pakistani War of 1971, which led to the creation of Bangladesh
  • Awarded Padma Vibhushan, India’s second-highest civilian award
  • Played a key role in World War II while serving in the British Indian Army
  • Known for excellent leadership, planning, and boosting soldiers’ morale
